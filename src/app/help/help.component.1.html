1. GETTING STARTED

Welcome to the Human Systems Integration Risk Management Tool (HSI-RMT). This software suite consists of two sub-tools designed to guide Human Systems Integration (HSI) analysts/practitioners, program managers, and Chief Engineers through the process of performing HSI best practices throughout system development.

The first sub-tool, the HSI Capabilities and Requirements Tool (HSI-CART), is designed to assist MAJCOM HSI Cells with the appropriate developmental planning and pre-Joint Capabilities Integration Development System (JCIDS) activities, such as Concept Development and Analysis of Alternatives.

The second, the Program Risk Assessment Tool, is designed to assist the system program office with integrating HSI throughout the JCIDS process and acquisition life cycle, from Material Solution Analysis to Disposal planning.

In this first section, we will introduce HSI and how to get started using the tool by launching the tool, creating an account, signing into the HSI-RMT, adding a project, and selecting a tool and questionnaire. In the second section, we will cover how to use the HSI-RMT, which includes editing projects, answering questions, creating and editing integration eveluations, reviewing and printing reports, editing your profile, and logging out of the tool. Keyboard shortcuts will be at the end of this manual.

1.1 Introduction to Human Systems Integration

AFI 63-1201, Life Cycle Systems Engineering, defines Human Systems Integration as “a disciplined, unified, and interactive systems engineering approach to integrate human considerations into the system development, design, and life cycle management to improve total system performance and reduce total costs of ownership.” Those human considerations span nine U.S. Air Force recognized HSI domains:

  1. Human Factors Engineering – Design characteristics for human capabilities and limitations.
  2. Manpower – Assessment of the human capital requirements (manning mix) necessary to operate, maintain, and support the system.
  3. Personnel – Assessment of the attributes (knowledge, skills, and abilities) required to operate, maintain, and support the system.
  4. Training – design and development of instruction, resources, and support required to impart system end users with the required knowledge, skills, and abilities necessary to operate, maintain, and support the system.
  5. Environment – Assessment of those operational environmental factors and how they impact system design, as well as how the design impacts the operational environment.
  6. Safety – Assessment of system design characteristics and associated procedures that serve to minimize mishap potential.
  7. Occupational Health – Assessment of system design features that serve to minimize the risks for injury, illness, or disability, and enhance job performance.
  8. Survivability – Consideration of system design features which reduce the likelihood for death or serious injury, to include consideration for hostile environments, fratricide, and emergency egress.
  9. Habitability – Integrated considerations of the system and associated facilities’ impact social interactions, morale, quality of life, safety and comfort, etc. to sustain maximum performance.

Human Systems Integration is a unique discipline in that is it both a technical and managerial philosophy. Per Department of Defense Instruction 5000.02, program managers are required to plan for and implement HSI through the product lifecycle. The earlier HSI is introduced, the more likely it is to reap the long-term benefits of risk mitigation, reduced redesign and rework, mishap reduction, etc. and achieve the desired life cycle cost savings. If properly included in system development, analysts and practitioners integrate and examine the risks and tradeoffs amongst the nine domains balance against life cycle cost considerations to provide consultation and decision making support to the program manager, ensuring that human considerations are adequately accounted for. In this manner, developers are able to drive total system performance and minimize total life cycle cost.

HSI is not just another name for Human Factors or just good systems engineering. Rather, Human Factors is just one of the disciplines that analysts integrates – HSI includes much more. In fact, a primary role of the analyst/practitioner is to integrate each of the HSI domains as well as those from other functional leads to advise program managers and systems engineers on the integrated tradeoffs.

2. USING THE HSI RISK MANAGEMENT TOOL

2.1 Editing Projects

Click the "Project" menu item at the top of the screen to return to the Projects page. Your projects will be listed here. The list is sorted by the time projects were last modified. You won't have to search for the project you are currently working on, because it will be at the top of the list. Shown below is the one project entered in this tutorial.

There are four actions you can take to work on a project: return to the last edited questionnaire, switch questionnaires, edit the project title and description, or archive a project.

Figure 11. Add Project Dialog

2.1.1 Return to the Last Edited Questionnaire

Click the project title, and you will be returned to the last question you were answering (in the most recently accessed questionnaire).

2.1.2 Switch Questionnaires

There are several links you can click to change questionnaires. You can access the Tools page by clicking either the "Tools" button or menu item, pick a tool (CART or PRAT), then choose a new questionnaire from the eight options shown on those two pages. You can, of course, click either the "CART" or "PRAT" menu items to got directly to those two pages, then choose a new questionnaire.

When you switch, you will be returned to the last question you were editing in that questionnaire.

2.1.3 Edit the Project Title and Description

Click the Edit button to bring up the "Edit project details" dialog (as shown below). The title and description will already be entered.

Figure 12. Edit Project Details Dialog

Make your changes and click save. You can also clear all the content and start over, but only the description is optional. You must enter a title. Alternatively, you can discard all changes by clicking the red minus button at in the lower right corner of the page.

Figure 13. Edit Project Details Dialog with changes

Changes are saved to disk and shown immediately in the list.

Figure 14. Revised project list

2.1.4 Archive a Project

Click the "Archive" button to archive a project. Archiving a project allows you to clean up your project list by reducing the amount of projects in your list. To view the archived projects, click the "Show archives" hyperlink located above the first project on the right side.

Figure 15. Archiving a project with "Show archives"

Once you are in the Archived projects page, click the "Restore" button of any project to return it to the active projects list. Return to the active projects list by clicking the "Show active projects" hyperlink located above the first project on the right side. Note: you can not view or modify a project when it has been archived. You must restore it to the active projects to view and edit your inputs.

Since restoration modifies the project, it will now appear at the top of the active project list.

Figure 16. Archives page

2.2 Answering Questions

Once you have selected a questionnaire, you will return to the last question you were working on in that questionnaire. If you have not answered any questions, you will be shown the first question of the General domain (as shown below)

Figure 17. Question Page

NOTE: there is no "Save" button for your inputs. Changes are automatically saved.

2.2.1 Answering a Question Yes

When you click the "Yes" button, the "Rationale" text box will open up across the screen. Writing a rationale is OPTIONAL.

Figure 18. Selecting YES to a Question

2.2.2 Answering a Question No

When you click the "No" button, a "Risk Matrix" and a "Description of Risks" text box appears on the screen.

Figure 19. Selecting NO to a question

2.2.2.1 Selecting a Risk Level

When you click on "Green" or "Low" risk box in the "Risk Matrix", two things occur:

  • A number is automatically assigned to that level of risk
  • The "Description of Risks" widens across the screen

When you click on "Yellow/Red" or "Moderate/High" risk box in the "Risk Matrix", three things occur:

  • A number is automatically assigned to that level of risk
  • The "Description of Risks" widens across the screen
  • A "Risk Planning" label with a down arrow appears

Within the "Description or Risks", you will be prompted to identify and prioritize risks associated with that HSI question. The selected risk level should correspond to the most important risk. As the project progresses, you can update the status of risks and the risk matrix, as necessary.

Figure 20. Selected "Red/High" Risk Level to a question

When you click the Risk Planning down arrow, a set of risk planning questions appear.

2.2.2.2 Risk Planning

The first question that appears determines how you plan to handle the risk. If you select "Accept" or "Transfer", then you continue to answer the questions regarding:

  • Risk impact affecting primarily cost, schedule, and performance
  • Whether or not appropriate stakeholders were informed
  • Whether or not the mitigation strategy and plan been documented and presented at all relevant decision points

Figure 21. "Accept" or "Transfer" Risk Planning

If you select "Avoid" or "Control", an additional two questions with text boxes appear. These questions address the following:

  • Specifying the risk mitigation plan
  • Monitoring and tracking the mitigation plan

Figure 22. "Avoid" or "Control" Risk Planning

2.2.3 Navigating the Questionnaire

Next and Previous Questions

There are two ways to navigate between questions. First, you can click the "Left Arrow" button to move to the previous question and the "Right Arrow" button to move to the next question. Second, you can use keyboard shortcuts. Click the corresponding left/right arrow keys on your keyboard to navigate between pages.

Question Menu

The Question Menu displays a list of questions for each corresponding domain. The Question Menu provides quick access for viewing and jumping to questions.

There are two ways to activate the Question Menu. First, you can click the corresponding "Down Arrow" located next to the title of the selected Domain Tab. Second, you can click the corresponding down arrow key on your keyboard to initiate the Question Menu.

Figure 23. Question Menu

There are a few ways to navigate the Question Menu:

  • Selecting a question with your mouse/trackpad
  • Clicking on the "Up/Left" and "Down/Right" arrows on your keyboard

As you answer the questionnaire, the Question Menu updates with icons to indicate what questions have been answered. If you answered Yes, you are given a green check mark icon. If you answered No, then it shows a red X icon. If you answered No and selected a risk level, then the a green, yellow, or red icon will be displayed. If you wrote something in the "Rationale" or "Description of Risks" text boxes, there will be a green pencil icon.

Figure 24. Question Menu with questions answered

Domain Tabs

The Domain Tabs allow you to jump between domain questions. You can navigate between Domain tabs in two ways:

  • Selecting a Domain Tab with your mouse/trackpad
  • Pressing the keyboard "Control" + "Left/Right" keys
  • Pressing the "<" + ">" keys

NOTE: If the Question Menu is open, you can navigate through the Domain Tabs using the same Domain Tabs navigational methods. The list of questions with corresponding status icons are shown for each tab.

2.2.4 Changing an Answer to NA

Unanswered questions will be recorded as NA (Not Applicable). If you accidentally answer a question that does not apply, you can reset the answer by clicking the keyboard "Control" + "Backspace/Delete" keys.

Once you click the "Integrations" menu item in the top Navigation Bar, you will be taken to the Integration Trade-Off List page. This page allows you to create, select, modify, and archive Integration Trade-Offs. If you have not yet created an integration evaluation, you will see the message shown below.

There are two shortcut buttons: "MPT" and "ESOH" (see Figure 26). 2.4 Reviewing and Printing Reports

General

Click the Review menu item in the top Navigation Bar to go to the Review page. The Review Page displays all of your input from the questionnaire. There are separate sections for "Questions and Answers" and "Trade-Off Analysis". Questions/Answers can be sorted in three ways:

  • By risk only
  • By domain, then risk
  • By domain - all questions

Sorted "By risk only"

The "By risk only" sort order displays all risks from highest to lowest risk level regardless of domain. This is also the default sort setting. If there are multiple answers with the same risk level number, then they are sorted in numerical order (Domain/Tab sequence number, then question number).

Figure 30. Review: By risk only

Sorted "By Domain, then risk"

The "By Domain, then risk" sort order displays risks sorted first by domain sequence number, then from highest risk level to lowest within that domain. If there are multiple answers with the same risk level (1 through 25), then the question/answers within that ranking are sorted in numerical order.

Sorted "By Domain - all questions"

The "By Domain - all questions" sort order displays all questions and answers in numerical order (first by Domain/Tab sequence number, then question number).

Printing

Click the green printer button to print a PDF output of the your review.
